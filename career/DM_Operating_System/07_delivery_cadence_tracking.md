# Function 7: Delivery Cadence & Tracking
*Sprint/milestone rhythm, progress measurement, and forecasting*

**Principle:** Delivery without tracking is hope without evidence. Cadence creates predictability. Tracking creates visibility. Forecasting creates credibility.

---

## Method

*At Project Start:*
- Define the delivery rhythm: sprint length or milestone dates, review cadence, when stakeholders see progress
- Make this explicit and share it — the team should not have to guess when things are due

*Daily Tracking:*
- Maintain a personal calendar of next actions per project: what is the next thing that needs to happen, who owns it, by when
- If an action depends on someone else: obtain a commitment date. Follow up on that date. If no date is given, follow up daily.
- When velocity slips (actions completing later than planned), treat this as a schedule risk immediately — not at the milestone

*Add One Leading Indicator:*
Current tracking is lagging (next actions, tasks completed). Add one leading signal:
- **Blocker count:** How many items are currently blocked and how long have they been blocked?
- Rising blocker count = leading indicator of a schedule problem before it materializes in missed dates

*Shared Status Artifact:*
- Maintain one visible project status that any stakeholder can check without asking
- Minimum: current milestone, % complete estimate, status (Green/Amber/Red), last updated date
- In OL: use the internal tool's status fields for this. In any other context: a shared document or tracker.

---

## Cadence

Delivery rhythm defined at project start. Daily personal next-action review. Weekly status artifact update.

---

## Artifact

Personal next-action calendar (informal). Shared project status with RAG (Red/Amber/Green) and last-updated date.

---

## Adaptation Notes

| Context | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Agile sprint-based | Velocity, sprint goals, and burn-down are primary tracking tools |
| Waterfall / phase-gate | Milestone and deliverable completion is the tracking currency |
| Outsourcing / client-facing | Client-visible tracking artifact required (weekly report, dashboard, shared project tracker) |
| Multiple parallel streams | Program-level view needed; individual stream tracking rolls up |

---

## Self-Check

- Is there a shared status artifact that a stakeholder could read right now without contacting anyone?
- How many items are currently blocked, and since when?
- Does the current velocity reach the next milestone on time?

---

## Gap to Close

Add the blocker count as a standing item in the weekly status artifact. Even one number — "3 items currently blocked" — turns an invisible risk into a visible signal.
