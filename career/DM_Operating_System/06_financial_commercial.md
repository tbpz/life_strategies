# Function 6: Financial & Commercial Awareness
*Budget, burn rate, contract basics, and knowing when numbers are off*

**Principle:** In outsourcing contexts, DMs are often closest to both the client relationship and the financials. Not understanding the commercial model means being blind to the biggest delivery risk — the one that triggers contract disputes and early terminations.

**Current State:** This function is almost entirely undeveloped due to a product company background. Financial metrics are not tracked; contract structures are unfamiliar. This is a genuine knowledge gap, not a documentation gap.

---

## Baseline to Build

*Contract Structures (study required):*
- **Fixed-price:** Scope is locked; every change is a revenue/cost event. Scope creep without a change order = margin erosion.
- **T&M (Time & Materials):** Billing is hourly/daily; focus is on billing accuracy and client visibility into hours consumed.
- **Retainer:** Recurring capacity commitment; focus on utilization and whether value matches the retainer cost.

*Burn Rate Awareness (to develop):*
- Know the approximate budget for any project being managed
- Track at a simple level: how much has been spent (hours × rate, or cost to date) vs. budget remaining vs. work remaining
- When these three are not proportional, that is a financial risk — flag it, do not absorb it

*Triggers for Commercial Action:*
- Scope expanding without a change order → raise a change order
- Timeline slipping → understand if there is a penalty clause before communicating delay
- Team effort not matching deliverables → investigate billing accuracy

---

## Cadence

Budget check monthly minimum. More frequently when scope changes are active or timeline is at risk.

---

## Artifact

Basic budget tracker (budget vs. spent vs. forecast). Change order log.

---

## Adaptation Notes

| Context | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Fixed-price contract | Budget management is the highest-stakes function; every untracked hour is margin erosion |
| T&M (time & materials) | Focus on billing accuracy and client transparency on hours consumed |
| Product company (internal) | Budget = headcount + vendor costs; focus on ROI framing rather than cost control |
| US/EU enterprise client | Finance reporting may be a contractual requirement with a defined format |

---

## Self-Check

- Is the contract structure (fixed-price / T&M / retainer) understood for the current engagement?
- Is the current budget vs. spent vs. forecast known?
- Has any scope expansion occurred without a corresponding change order?

---

## Gap to Close

This function requires deliberate study before it can be practiced. Study path:
1. Understand fixed-price vs. T&M in practice — read a real SOW or contract
2. Shadow a commercial conversation with a more experienced DM or account manager
3. Practice tracking budget vs. burn on a current or past project retrospectively
