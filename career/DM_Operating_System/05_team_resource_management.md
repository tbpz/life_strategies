# Function 5: Team & Resource Management
*How to lead, unblock, and organize the delivery team*

**Principle:** Delivery happens through people. Keeping the team unblocked, clear on their work, and correctly utilized is the most direct lever on output velocity.

---

## Method

*Role Clarity (Project Start):*
- At kick-off, confirm explicitly: what does each person own, who do they depend on, and what does "done" mean for their work
- Do not assume software development role clarity is universal — confirm it

*Blocker Detection:*
- Actively check for blockers between standups — a daily or every-other-day direct message to key executors: "anything blocking you?"
- When a blocker is identified, own its removal: escalate, negotiate with the dependency owner, or re-route the work

*Utilization:*
- Maintain awareness of who is overloaded vs. underutilized — visible through ticket count, self-report, and direct check-in
- Shift from daily micro check-ins toward a cadence of 2–3 times per week with brief written status per person. This reduces interruption while maintaining visibility.

*Onboarding New Team Members:*
A minimal onboarding checklist to run whenever someone joins:
1. Introduce project context and objective (15 min conversation)
2. Share the requirements document and RAID log
3. Introduce to key stakeholders and communication channels
4. Assign a first task with a clear acceptance criterion
5. Schedule a Day 3 and Day 7 check-in to surface questions

*Escalation Boundary:*
- **DM handles:** Work blockers, skill gaps on tasks, communication failures between team members, task re-prioritization, morale issues affecting delivery
- **Escalate to HR/leadership:** Conduct issues, persistent interpersonal conflict that has been attempted to resolve directly, capacity/resourcing decisions beyond DM authority

---

## Cadence

Blocker check every 1–2 days. Utilization review weekly. Onboarding checklist run for every new team member.

---

## Artifact

Onboarding checklist (reusable). Personal blocker log (informal note of what is blocked and since when).

---

## Adaptation Notes

| Context | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Outsourced / vendor team | Relationship management = a delivery input; invest in rapport early |
| Cross-cultural team | Communication norms, directness, and hierarchy expectations differ; map them explicitly |
| Remote-first | Async check-ins; documented decisions; deliberate informal touchpoints |
| Mixed seniority | Junior members need closer oversight; seniors need autonomy + clear outcomes |

---

## Self-Check

- Does every team member know what they own and what "done" looks like for their current work?
- Is there a new team member who has not received a structured onboarding?
- Is any blocker older than 2 days without active resolution?

---

## Gap to Close

Reduce micro-check-in frequency deliberately. Replace "ask everyone daily" with "async status + escalate only when status deviates from plan."
