# Function 1: Engagement Setup
*What to do in Week 1 of any new project or company*

**Principle:** The first two weeks set the operational baseline for everything that follows. Skipping structured engagement means spending months in reactive firefighting. Most delivery failures in the first quarter trace back to Week 1 foundations that were assumed, not established.

---

## Method

*Day 1–2: Stakeholder Mapping*
- List every relevant stakeholder by name and role
- For each: What is their interest in this project? What authority do they hold? What information do they control? Who do they influence informally (beyond their official role)?
- Identify the central authority/information point (usually PM or equivalent) and the technical feasibility source (usually Tech Lead)
- Write this down — even as a simple table. It does not live in memory

*Day 1–3: Artifact Location*
- Locate or create: requirements/scope document, backlog or task list, team roster, any contract or SOW if applicable
- If an artifact does not exist, that absence is itself a risk — flag it immediately

*Week 1: Listening Sessions*
- Hold a direct conversation (not a status meeting) with the PM, the Tech Lead, and at least one other key stakeholder
- Goal: understand the project history, current state, known tensions, and any problems that exist but have not been named publicly
- Ask explicitly: "What's the thing we're most likely to get wrong here?"

*Week 1: Define "Done" for the First Milestone*
- Do not assume the requirements document is sufficient — requirements describe behavior, not success
- Get explicit alignment: "If we deliver X by Y date, what would need to be true for that to be considered a success?"
- Write the answer and share it back to the stakeholders for confirmation

*Week 1: Establish Communication Rhythm*
- Create the team communication channel (chat group, channel, etc.)
- Set or confirm the recurring sync cadence: what meetings exist, who attends, how often
- Even if the company culture is async/tool-based, define when direct verbal check-ins will happen and on what trigger

*Week 1: Map the Approval Chain*
- Identify who has authority to approve: scope changes, go-live decisions, resource additions, escalations
- Write it down. One sentence per decision type is enough.

---

## Cadence

Event-triggered. Run this checklist at the start of every new project, every new client, and every new company.

---

## Artifact

A simple Engagement Summary table (stakeholders + roles + authority + communication channel) and a confirmed "done" definition for the first milestone. Does not need to be elaborate — one document or a structured note is sufficient.

---

## Adaptation Notes

| Context | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| New company (not just project) | Extend stakeholder mapping to org chart and informal power — who are the real decision-makers vs. the official ones? |
| Inherited mid-flight project | Add a pre-mortem: what is already broken that no one is saying out loud? |
| Outsourcing / client-facing | Establish async protocol Day 1; identify the client's internal equivalent to DM role |
| Small team (≤5) | Lighter structure; direct relationships replace formal comms matrix |

---

## Self-Check

- Is the stakeholder map written down and current?
- Has "done" been explicitly confirmed with the person who will approve it?
- Do team members know the communication channel and cadence, or are they improvising?

---

## Gap to Close

Practice identifying the unspoken risk in Week 1 — the thing everyone knows but hasn't named. A useful prompt: "What would have to be true for this project to fail in a way that surprises everyone?"
