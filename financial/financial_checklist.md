# Pillar 2 — Financial Resilience Checklist

> **Purpose:** A one-time setup checklist to ensure financial resilience against income disruption, health incidents, and inflation erosion. Once complete, reviewed once per year maximum. Not an ongoing activity.

> **Disclaimer:** This is a personal planning document, not professional financial advice. All specific decisions should be validated against current Vietnam regulations and personal circumstances.

> **STATUS: ✅ CLOSED — 2026-05-15.** All three layers verified complete. Next review: annual only. No ongoing action needed.

## The Three Layers

### Layer 1: Emergency Buffer — ✅ Done
*Handles: job loss, health crisis, sudden large expense*

- [x] Calculate total monthly living expenses (all-in: rent, food, school, bills, etc.)
- [x] Target: **12 months** of expenses in liquid savings (12 months, not 6 — father with single income)
- [x] Verify: is the current bank savings balance above this 12-month target?
- [x] If yes → Layer 1 is done. Do not touch this portion for investment. ✅ Confirmed.
- [ ] If no → *(not applicable — buffer confirmed above target)*

**Storage:** Multiple bank savings accounts at 5–7% interest. This is correct for this layer. No changes needed.

---

### Layer 2: Surplus Savings — ✅ Done
*Handles: long-term purchasing power preservation*

**Decision (final):** All surplus savings stay in bank deposits. No ETF, no stocks, no gold.

**Why this is enough:** Vietnam bank deposits at 5–7% consistently beat inflation (3–4%) every year for the past decade, delivering +1–3% real return annually. This is genuine wealth preservation — not exciting, but real and reliable. The goal of this layer is not maximum returns; it is zero financial anxiety.

**Checklist:**

- [x] Calculate: Total savings minus 12 months living expenses = **surplus**
- [x] Park the surplus in a 12-month term deposit at a Big 4 bank (Vietcombank, BIDV, VietinBank, or Agribank) for the best available rate ✅ Already in term deposit.
- [x] Set a calendar reminder to re-deposit at maturity — no other action needed
- [x] Hard rule: do not touch this portion for any reason outside a true emergency

**Why Big 4 banks only:** State-owned or majority state-owned. The government has never let a Big 4 bank depositor lose money, and will not — the systemic risk is too high. Formal deposit insurance limit (125M VND) is irrelevant in practice for these banks.

**Allocation table:**
| Savings portion | Where |
|---|---|
| First 12 months of expenses | Demand/savings account — stays liquid |
| Everything above that | 12-month term deposit, Big 4 bank, roll over annually |

---

### Layer 3: Insurance — ✅ Done
*Handles: catastrophic health event, inability to work, death*

#### 3a. Health Coverage Audit
- [x] Review current employer health insurance (BHYT + any private supplement): what does it actually cover? ✅ PVI policy: inpatient 210M/year, outpatient 21M/year, accident death/disability 200M.
- [x] Identify the maximum out-of-pocket in a serious illness scenario ✅ Assessed.
- [x] If major gap exists: research — **No major gap requiring action.** Acute illness and hospitalization well covered. Critical illness lump sum not included in PVI, but gap is mitigated by Layer 1 emergency buffer. No private supplement needed.

#### 3b. Term Life Insurance (not whole life, not ILP)
**What to look for:** Pure risk coverage only. Pays out a lump sum if death or total permanent disability occurs. No savings component. No investment component. Premiums are low.

**What to avoid:** Whole life policies, investment-linked policies (ILPs) sold by agents — these mix bad investment products with insurance at high fees.

- [x] Run the self-insurance math first:
  - Savings cover 12+ months emergency buffer (Layer 1) + additional surplus in term deposit (Layer 2).
  - **Decision: self-insurance sufficient. Term life not pursued.**
- [ ] *(If proceeding: steps below — not applicable)*

#### 3c. Critical Illness Rider (optional but worth checking)
- [x] Check if current employer insurance or any existing policy includes critical illness coverage (pays lump sum on diagnosis of cancer, stroke, heart attack, etc.) ✅ Assessed — not included in PVI policy.
- [x] If not covered: **Gap mitigated by Layer 1 emergency buffer (12+ months liquid savings). No additional rider needed.**

## Annual Review (1 hour, once per year)

Scheduled alongside the career scan. Do not review more frequently.

- [ ] Is the emergency buffer still at 12 months? (Expenses may have changed)
- [ ] Is the surplus term deposit rolled over? (Should be automatic — just confirm once)
- [ ] Any major life change that affects insurance needs? (new child, significant salary change, etc.)
- [ ] Reconfirm: nothing else needs attention. Close the loop and return to Pillar 1.

---

## What Not to Do

- **Do not follow financial news or markets** — this feeds anxiety, not returns
- **Do not FOMO into ETFs, gold, or crypto** based on what people around you are doing — the strategy is decided, it is not up for review
- **Do not buy whole life / ILP products** from insurance agents — the investment component is a poor deal
- **Do not try to "optimize" beyond this system** — complexity is the enemy of consistency
- **Do not confuse 1986 hyperinflation stories with today's reality** — Vietnam bank deposits have beaten inflation every single year since 2013
