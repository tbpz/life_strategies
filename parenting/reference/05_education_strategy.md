# Education Strategy — Reference

The reasoning behind the chosen education approach. Read this when external pressure (from peers, family, or Vietnamese culture) creates doubt about the strategy.

---

## The Position

**Public school as the container. Home environment as the real education.**

---

## Why Public School (Not International)

- Public school in Vietnam provides adequate foundational academic content
- The user grew up through the public system and became a capable professional — personal validation that the system is sufficient
- International schools are expensive; their outcome advantage is largely a **selection effect**, not a treatment effect — they select for already-advantaged families, they don't create the advantage
- The real education happens at home: in how learning is framed, how curiosity is treated, and how failure is handled
- Homeschooling is a legal grey area in Vietnam and requires full parental involvement — not viable given the work schedule

---

## What "Home Supplementation" Means

This is **NOT tutoring.** It is about creating a home environment where:
- Questions are welcomed and explored, not dismissed
- Learning is modeled by parents (reading, building, problem-solving openly)
- The child sees adults struggle with things and work through them
- Books, materials, and experiences are available — but the child's **curiosity drives** what gets explored
- English exposure happens naturally (media, conversation, books) — not through drilling

The distinction: tutoring pushes content into the child. Home supplementation creates conditions where the child pulls learning toward themselves.

---

## What to Explicitly Reject

These are the common Vietnamese parenting moves that feel productive but are evidence-against:

| Rejected Practice | Why |
|---|---|
| Tutoring "to get ahead" before the child is behind | Academic advantage washes out by grade 3; creates pressure without durable gain |
| Multiple extracurricular classes to "build a portfolio" | Replaces unstructured play (where executive function develops) with scheduled performance |
| Comparing academic performance with peers as motivation | Trains external validation orientation; damages intrinsic motivation |
| Treating grades as the primary measure of development | Grades measure compliance with the school system, not capability, character, or curiosity |
| Sacrificing play, rest, or family time for additional study | Sleep and play are not optional — they are when learning consolidates |

---

## The Meta-Learning Edge

The family cannot compete with elite households on resources (tutors, programs, connections). The competitive strategy is therefore:

**Build a child who can learn anything, rather than a child who has been taught everything.**

This means:
- Metacognition: thinking about one's own thinking, recognizing when a strategy isn't working
- Growth mindset: effort and strategy, not innate talent, determine outcomes
- Intrinsic motivation: curiosity-driven learning, not grade-driven performance
- Resilience: productive failure is how learning happens, not a sign of inadequacy

A child with these internal capabilities will outcompete a tutored child by the time the academic content gets hard enough that drilling fails — typically mid-high school or university.
