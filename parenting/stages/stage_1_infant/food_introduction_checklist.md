# Food Introduction Checklist

**Goal:** Expose son to all major food types, allergens, and textures **before 12 months** (~3 months runway from May 2026).

**How to use:** Work through the list at whatever pace fits the family. One new food per day is ideal, but no pressure. The only hard rules:
- Introduce allergens in the **morning** (monitor for 2 hours after)
- Wait **2–3 days between new allergens** (so if there's a reaction, you know which food caused it)
- Non-allergen foods (vegetables, fruits) can be introduced daily with no waiting period
- **Rejected food ≠ failed food** — it takes 8–15 exposures before a child accepts a new taste. Re-offer later.

**No family allergy history** — all allergens can be introduced at home without prior pediatrician clearance.

## Texture Progression

Goal: transition from fully smooth → finger foods before 12 months.

| ✓ | Texture Level | Examples | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| ✅ | **Fully smooth** (current) | Everything puréed and mixed into cháo | Done |
| | **Soft mash with lumps** | Fork-mashed banana, roughly mashed sweet potato, cháo with visible food pieces | |
| | **Soft finger foods** | Steamed carrot sticks, ripe banana pieces, soft tofu cubes, cooked pumpkin chunks | |
| | **Varied textures in one meal** | Cháo with chunky vegetables + finger food on the side | |

**Note on gagging:** Gagging is normal — it's the body's safety reflex pushing food forward. It's loud and the child recovers. Choking is silent, no sound, face color changes. Learn the difference before starting finger foods. Ask the pediatrician to demonstrate or watch a verified instructional video.

## Safety Reminders

- **Morning introductions for allergens** — 2 hour monitoring window
- **Watch for:** hives, swelling (lips/face), vomiting, difficulty breathing
- **Mild reactions** (flushed cheeks, slight rash around mouth) are common and usually not dangerous
- **Severe reaction** → emergency room immediately
- **Never give whole nuts, grapes, or round hard foods** — choking hazard. Always mash, slice, or grind.
- **No honey before 12 months** — botulism risk
- **No cow's milk as a drink before 12 months** — formula or breast milk remains the main liquid
