---

## Why Nanny First, Daycare Second

- Baby under 12 months → most quality daycare won't accept yet
- Group care means constant illness → triggers the exact gap we're trying to prevent
- At this age, 1:1 home care with cameras = safer and more controllable than institutional care
- Daycare waitlists are 1-3 months → research now, but don't expect it to solve today's problem
- **Splitting focus 50/50 now means neither track gets done well**

Daycare becomes the stronger option at **18-24 months**. Until then: nanny is the backbone, daycare is the next evolution.

## Phase 1 — This Week: Find the Nanny

*Window: while bà ngoại is here to supervise. Don't waste it.*

- [ ] Research 3+ reputable nanny agencies in HCMC (Day 2)
- [ ] Write baby care protocol: feeding, nap schedule, fever plan, emergency contacts (Day 3)
- [ ] Contact 2-3 agencies, request candidate profiles (Day 3-4)
- [ ] Refine [acceptance criteria](nanny_acceptance_criteria.md) with real context *(draft — revisit after first agency calls)*
- [ ] Draft interview plan *(pending — finalize once candidates are shortlisted)*
- [ ] Schedule interviews for end of Week 1 or early Week 2 (Day 4-5)

**Daycare this week:** 1-2 hours max — browse, make a shortlist. Nothing more.

---

## Phase 2 — Weeks 2-4: Trial the Nanny

- [ ] Interview at least 3 candidates. Keep #2 contact warm — always
- [ ] Run supervised trial with bà ngoại present (Week 1 of trial: she observes)
- [ ] Run semi-supervised trial (Week 2: bà ngoại home but disengaged)
- [ ] Review camera footage regularly. Debrief with nanny weekly.
- [ ] Nanny passes → bà ngoại can return to Hanoi with a system in place

**Daycare this phase:** Visit 1-2 facilities on a weekend. Get a feel for quality.

**Nanny screening — non-negotiables:**
- References from previous families (call them directly)
- Experience with infants under 12 months
- Comfortable with cameras (cameras are already installed — gauge reaction when informed)
- Engages with written care protocol — doesn't gloss over it

---

## Phase 3 — Months 2-3: Stabilize

- [ ] Nanny is trusted and providing solo care
- [ ] Bà nội (MIL) returns — light/optional role only, no daily obligation
- [ ] Remote days reserved strictly for sick days and emergencies
- [ ] Daycare research active: visit top candidates, get on waitlists

---

## Phase 4 — Months 4-6: Daycare Transition Prep

- [ ] Apply / join waitlist at 1-2 preferred facilities
- [ ] Decide: full daycare or daycare + part-time nanny hybrid at 12-18 months
- [ ] Keep nanny through transition as a backup layer

---

## Key Risks

| Risk | Action |
|------|--------|
| Bà ngoại recalled to Hanoi suddenly | Nanny search is a sprint, not a project. Start now. |
| First nanny doesn't work out | Always have backup candidate ready to call |
| Bà nội pushes to return as primary | Redirect to light role — define it specifically so she feels useful |
| Baby sick → can't go anywhere | This is what remote days are for. Reserve them. |
