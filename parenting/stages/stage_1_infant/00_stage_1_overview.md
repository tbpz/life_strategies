# Stage 1: Infant (0–18 months) — CURRENT

**Status:** ✅ Active — Son is 9 months old (May 2026)

**Core insight:** Brain is wiring for trust, language, and emotional regulation. This happens through warm, present, repeated interaction — not stimulation programs or structured teaching. Repetition is the mechanism, not a limitation.

---

## Actions by Domain

| Domain | What to Do Now |
|---|---|
| 🧠 Cognitive | Narrate everything during daily care — "Now we're putting on pajamas. This is your left foot." |
| 🗣️ Language | Talk constantly. Sing. Read aloud. Vocabulary absorption starts before speech. |
| ❤️ Social-Emotional | Respond to every signal warmly and consistently. Physical play (bouncing, tickling) on weekends. |
| 🏃 Physical | Tummy time daily (≥30 min). Floor time — let the child crawl and explore freely. |
| 🏥 Health | Zero screens (WHO: under 18mo). 12–16h sleep. Food diversity before 12mo → [checklist](stages/stage_1_infant/food_introduction_checklist.md). |
| ⚖️ Moral | Consistent, warm responses teach: "the world is predictable and safe." |

**Parenting focus:** Establish a bedtime/bath-time ritual (15–20 min, same sequence every night, phones away).

**Do NOT:** flashcards, teaching sessions, screen time, milestone comparison.

## Weekly Execution Check (2 min, Sunday)

Ask both parents. Gut answer only — no scoring.

- [ ] Did the bedtime/bath ritual happen most nights this week?
- [ ] Were we talking and narrating during care moments?
- [ ] Were phones away during the ritual?

**If 2+ are No two weeks in a row** — something in the routine needs to change, not just more effort.

---

## Developmental Health Check

Run at each pediatric checkup. These are "nothing alarming" signals, not achievement targets. If most are yes, the child is on track. Any persistent No → mention to the pediatrician.

**At 9 months:**
- [ ] Looks where you point?
- [ ] Babbles with different sounds (ba-ba, da-da)?
- [ ] Responds to own name?
- [ ] Shows joy with familiar people?
- [ ] Sits without support?

**At 12 months:**
- [ ] Pulls to stand?
- [ ] Says 1–2 words beyond mama/dada?
- [ ] Waves bye-bye or claps?
- [ ] Looks for hidden objects?
- [ ] Lights up when a parent enters the room? *(the most important signal — secure attachment forming)*

**At 18 months:**
- [ ] Walking independently?
- [ ] Says 5–10 words?
- [ ] Points to show interest in things?
- [ ] Follows simple 1-step instructions?
- [ ] Shows affection to familiar people?

---

## When to Escalate

- **2+ milestone items persistently No** at any checkpoint → raise with pediatrician at next visit, don't wait
- **Child loses a skill they already had** (regression) → flag to pediatrician promptly
- **Either parent's gut says something is off** → trust it and consult — early intervention is always more effective than late intervention
