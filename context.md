## Core Identity & Values
*These values were distilled through guided self-reflection. They represent the non-negotiable principles that drive decisions.*

- **Purpose & Meaning:** Work and life pursuits must feel genuinely meaningful. Refuses to chase goals (titles, salary, status) that don't connect to a personal "why." Won't run a race whose finish line holds no real desire.
- **Autonomy:** Needs to be self-directed. Rejects blind authority and orders without rational justification. Decisions must come from internal conviction, not external pressure or fear of judgment.
- **Intrinsic Joy (Fun through Progress):** Energy comes from the process itself—constant small wins and tangible progress. Financial outcomes are secondary; the work itself must be enjoyable. Willing to endure temporarily unfun work *only* if it's a clearly reasoned stepping stone.
- **Competence & Mastery:** Deep need for genuine capability and to be recognized for actual skill, especially in high-confidence areas. Values substance over appearance.
- **Authenticity:** All major decisions must originate from genuine personal desire, not from social expectations, fear, or external validation.
- **Rational Adaptability:** Commits genuinely based on the best logical reasoning available at the time. However, stays fully willing to pivot without guilt when real-world experience reveals a misalignment. No sunk-cost attachment—initial beliefs were correct given the information at that moment; growth simply changes the equation.

## Current Context
*Snapshot of current life situation as of April 2026.*

### Career & Work
- **Current Role:** Tech professional (product development), deliberately chose a less demanding role after leaving a 9-year position at a beloved company.
- **Capacity Usage:** Operating at ~70% capability. The job has clear process and scope, freeing mental bandwidth for personal exploration.
- **Strategic Intent:** The job is a *platform*, not a destination—it funds life while providing the 30% mental space needed to pursue the real goal.
- **Growing Tension:** Being underutilized is starting to feel like a drain. The job is only tolerable when the exploration side shows progress.

### Life Stage & Financial
- **Age:** Mid-30s.
- **Family:** Has a family to provide for. Financial decisions carry responsibility beyond self.
- **Financial Status:** Comfortable middle class. Owns a house. But not large enough to feel secure against 15-20 year black swan events (war, inflation, health crises). Financial anxiety exists beneath the surface comfort.

### The Creative Direction (The Real Goal)
- **Ultimate Desire:** To create emotionally powerful, deeply touching stories—the kind of masterpieces (in anime, animation, manga, games) that reach into people and change something inside them.
- **Origin:** A lifelong consumer and connoisseur of great storytelling across all media. Realized the world doesn't have enough of these works, and consumption alone isn't sustainable. Decided to become a creator.
- **Aspired Role:** **Original Story Creator (原作者)** — the person from whose mind the story, world, and characters emerge. Ownership is intellectual and emotional, not necessarily technical. Comfortable delegating craft execution (art, animation, music) to specialists, as long as the final output serves the original vision. Not a manager/coordinator (that feels like non-contribution) and not a pure auteur (doing everything solo is unrealistic at scale). The measure of success: people are deeply moved, and they know it was the *story* that moved them.
- **Medium Priority:** Animation = Anime = Manga > Game > Movie > Novel. Prioritizes media that allow full expressive control (visuals, sound, movement) without dependency on others' performance.
- **Current State of Progress:** Feels very vague and directionless. Has tried some creative directions but visible progress toward the dream is minimal.

### Core Tension
> The entire system runs on **progress as fuel**. The job is tolerable because it funds exploration. But the exploration isn't producing visible wins. Without small progress, both sides feel hollow—and the comfort window is ticking. The #1 strategic need is to convert "vague exploration" into a path with measurable small wins.

## Strategic Pillars
*Three pillars, ordered by priority. Everything serves the first.*

### Governing Principle: Art-First, Commerce-Second
> The creative work must always serve the artistic vision—never be driven by financial incentive. The moment creation is done *for* money, it becomes another race with an undesired finish line. Tech/career remains the financial backbone. If the creative work *naturally* generates income, that's welcome—but it must never dictate creative direction.

### Pillar 1: 🎨 Creative Mastery (The Dream)
*To become an Original Story Creator capable of crafting emotionally powerful stories that reach people through animation/anime/manga.*
- **Current State:** Early exploration, vague direction, minimal visible progress.
- **Key Challenge:** The gap between "tech professional" and "master storyteller" feels enormous. No clear step-by-step path yet. Without visible small wins, motivation stalls.
- **Primary Craft to Master:** Storytelling — narrative structure, character development, emotional arc design, world-building. This is the core skill, not animation or drawing.
- **What Needs to Happen:** Break storytelling mastery into concrete, learnable sub-skills and build a practice system that generates regular small wins. Visual/technical craft is secondary and can be delegated or AI-assisted.
- **Relationship to Other Pillars:** This is the reason everything else exists. The other pillars are infrastructure that buys time and resources for this one.

### Pillar 2: 💰 Career & Financial Resilience (The Engine)
*To maintain and grow financial stability enough to sustain family, lifestyle, and the creative pursuit long-term.*
- **Current State:** 10-year PM/DM professional. Previously at Zalo (major chat platform, 9 years), now Delivery Manager at Orange Logic (enterprise DAM, US/EU clients). Stable but deliberately operating at 70%. Income comfortable, savings cover several years of basic expenses.
- **Core Strength:** Execution — getting things actually done. Full product lifecycle understanding (ideation → delivery → operation) gives a competitive edge even in a delivery-only role.
- **Key Challenge:** Career anxiety driven by a confidence wound from Zalo, not by actual market risk. Tried senior strategic ownership at Zalo, struggled under the weight of organizational accountability, and retreated. Now stuck: the traditional growth path (PM → Director → VP) triggers trauma, but staying still feels like decay. The real fear isn't job loss — it's deprecation over time as the industry evolves.
- **Strategic Decision (Locked):** Career = engine only, never the destination. Stop searching for a job that merges income with creative dream (Option 2). Commit fully to keeping income and creative pursuit separate (Option 1). Growth direction is **lateral capability building** (becoming AI-augmented, strengthening unique profile) — not vertical ladder climbing.
- **Security Threshold:** Ability to find a comparable role within 2 months if needed. Achieved through marketability maintenance (professional presence, network, current skills), not through title advancement.
- **Relationship to Other Pillars:** Funds everything. The sprint goal is to define "good enough" and build a minimal safety net, then close the loop and redirect energy to Pillar 1.
- **Deep Dive:** See `pillar_2_career.md` for full analysis, root causes, and action plan.

### Pillar 3: 👦 Fatherhood (The Responsibility & Desire)
*To raise his young son to become a good person.*
- **Current State:** Son is very young. Parenting direction and strategies are unclear.
- **Key Challenge:** Wants to be intentional about parenting but hasn't defined what "raising him properly" concretely means yet.
- **What Needs to Happen:** Define parenting values and principles. Build intentional habits and rituals. Explore whether the creative pursuit can also serve as a bonding and teaching vehicle with his son.
- **Relationship to Other Pillars:** Adds weight to financial stability requirements. Could potentially intersect with the creative dream (storytelling as a parenting tool, creating works *for* his son).

## Goals & Milestones

### Sequencing Strategy
The three pillars are not tackled simultaneously with equal weight. They operate on different timescales and must be sequenced to avoid paralysis:

> **The core problem:** Pillar 2's unresolved anxiety contaminates focus on Pillar 1. Fixing the anxiety first (even imperfectly) unlocks the creative exploration.

| Phase | Pillar | Mode | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Now → 4 weeks** | 💰 Career/Financial | Focused sprint | Pick a *tentative* growth direction. Not to solve forever—just enough to stop the anxiety. |
| **After sprint → Ongoing** | 🎨 Creative Mastery | Primary exploration | With career anxiety reduced, redirect 30% free energy here. Break the dream into learnable skills with regular small wins. |
| **Always** | 👦 Fatherhood | Always-on, daily habits | Not a project to schedule—a set of clear principles and daily presence. |

### Short-term (Now → 3 months)
- [ ] **[Pillar 2]** Run a focused career direction sprint: identify a tech growth path that keeps skills marketable without consuming creative energy. Pick a tentative direction—apply Rational Adaptability, pivot later if needed.
- [ ] **[Pillar 3]** Define 3-5 core parenting principles. Establish at least one consistent daily or weekly ritual with son.
- [ ] **[Pillar 1]** After career sprint: map the skill gap between current self and creative goal. Identify the *first* learnable skill to develop (likely: storytelling/writing OR visual fundamentals).

### Medium-term (3 months → 2 years)
- [ ] **[Pillar 1]** Establish a consistent creative practice with visible, measurable small wins (e.g., complete short stories, storyboards, or a short animation experiment).
- [ ] **[Pillar 2]** Execute on chosen career growth direction. Achieve a tangible marker of progress (new skill certified, freelance project, promotion, or side income stream).
- [ ] **[Pillar 1+2]** Explore whether tech skills (AI, generative media, production pipelines) can serve the creative dream—without letting commerce pollute the creative vision.

### Long-term (2–10 years)
- **[Pillar 1]** Complete and release at least one meaningful creative work (short film, animated short, manga chapter, or game demo) that represents the vision.
- **[Pillar 2]** Financial situation is secure enough to absorb black swan events over a 15-20 year horizon.
- **[Pillar 3]** Son is growing with clear values, a strong relationship with his father, and a sense of being seen and supported.

## Constraints & Challenges
*Known blockers and tensions that strategies must account for.*

- **Vagueness as the primary enemy:** All three pillars currently lack concrete direction. Without clear next steps, progress stalls and motivation collapses—especially for someone whose engine runs on visible small wins.
- **Career confidence wound:** The Zalo experience left a scar — strategic failure under organizational pressure created a fear of the traditional growth path. The anxiety about career safety is disproportionate to actual risk (10yr experience, international exposure, several years of savings). The real work is psychological: defining "good enough" and giving permission to stop worrying.
- **Financial anxiety beneath the surface:** Despite being comfortable today, the 15-20 year horizon feels insecure. This is partly rational (black swan events) but partly a pattern — the anxiety will find new things to attach to regardless of objective safety.
- **The art-vs-commerce tension:** Convergence between tech career and creative dream is possible but dangerous. Must guard against financial motivation polluting creative direction.
- **The size of the dream:** The gap between current identity (tech professional) and desired identity (Original Story Creator of masterpieces) is enormous. This makes every small step feel insignificant, killing momentum.
- **Limited free energy:** Only ~30% of mental capacity is available for exploration. This must be protected and spent wisely—not scattered across all three pillars at once.
- **Fatherhood is non-negotiable daily:** Son's early years cannot be deferred. Parenting time and presence are fixed costs that the strategy must work around, not eliminate.

## AI Interaction Guidelines
*Instructions for any AI model working in this workspace.*

- **Role:** Strategic Advisor + Critical Challenger. Ask hard questions before giving comfortable answers. Surface tensions and blind spots. Don't moralize.
- **Communication Style:** Direct and concise. No generic motivational fluff. Challenge assumptions but respect the values already established in this document.
- **Context Handling:** Always read `context.md` first. When working on a specific pillar, also read the corresponding deep-dive file (e.g., `pillar_2_career.md`). Ground all advice in the 3 pillars and the sequencing strategy. Do not give generic life advice disconnected from this context.
- **Writing Conventions:** All workspace files (context.md, pillar files, any new files) must use **3rd person impersonal voice** — no "he/him/his" pronouns, no "I/my". Write as a neutral strategic briefing document about the subject. Example: "Deliberately chose Orange Logic" not "He chose Orange Logic" or "I chose Orange Logic". Apply this standard when creating new files or updating existing ones.
- **Guardrails:**
  - Never suggest creative work (Pillar 1) be driven by financial goals. Flag it immediately if a proposed direction risks polluting the creative vision with commercial incentives.
  - Never suggest climbing the traditional management ladder (PM → Director → VP) as the default career growth path. Lateral capability building is the chosen strategy.
  - The Video Generation Pipeline project (`d:\Tu\Video_Material_Generation_Workflow`) is a separate side project built for a friend's YouTube workflow. Do not conflate it with personal creative goals.
