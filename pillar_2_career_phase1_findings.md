**Key observations:**
- "Delivery Manager" as a title is sparse on local boards (<10 on ITviec), but the *function* is active under titles like "Engagement Manager," "Account Delivery Manager," "Tech Delivery Lead," "Program Manager."
- PM/DM is the **2nd most-recruited IT role** in Vietnam — 15.7% of demand (Navigos 2025), behind only developers.
- Market is **recovering**: LinkedIn global PM openings at 3-year high in 2025; "the most open PM roles in over two years" (Lenny's Newsletter 2026).

**Company type breakdown:**

| Company Type | Demand | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| IT Outsourcing / ODC (FPT Software, NashTech, KMS, Capgemini VN, GFT) | Very High | Largest employers of DMs; US/EU/JP client-facing |
| Product companies (Shopee, VNG, MoMo, Tiki) | High | Shift from outsourcing to product-led model accelerating |
| FDI tech hubs (Bosch, Siemens, Intel Vietnam) | High | Digital delivery hubs expanding |
| Enterprise IT / Banking / Fintech | Moderate-High | Digital transformation wave |
| Startups | Moderate | Fewer formal DM roles; hybrid PM/delivery titles |

**Most common job requirements:**
- 5–10 years experience for Senior PM; 6–10 for Senior DM
- Business English fluency: near-universal; "international client communication" explicit in many postings
- Technical literacy: basic SQL, architecture understanding, agile tooling (Jira, Confluence)
- Certifications: PMP in ~30–40% of DM postings; Agile/CSM/SAFe frequently mentioned
- AI tool familiarity: emerging requirement appearing in 2025–2026 postings

## Finding 4: AI Impact on PM/DM Roles

**Functions being automated / AI-assisted:**
- Backlog prioritization, user story drafting, PRD generation from prompts
- Meeting summaries and action item extraction
- Risk flagging and predictive scheduling
- Sprint reporting, burndown, competitive research synthesis

**Usage data (PMI 2025 survey):**
- 54% of PMs now use AI for risk management
- 53% for task automation; 52% for predictive analysis
- 20–30% time savings reported on routine tasks
- Only 20% of PMs have sufficient AI experience to fully leverage it — **gap = advantage for early adopters**

**New skills expected because of AI:**

| Category | Specifics |
|---|---|
| AI Literacy | Prompt engineering for specs/research; knowing when AI output is wrong |
| Data-led decisions | SQL basics, Amplitude/Mixpanel/GA4, A/B test interpretation |
| AI product management | Building AI features; managing hallucination/bias/latency risks |
| Workflow automation | Designing AI-augmented PM/DM workflows |
| Strategic communication | Translating AI capabilities to non-technical executives and clients |

**Demand trend: Recovering and growing.** Global PM openings back to 3-year highs in 2025. AI PM roles specifically growing 28% to 2030. PMI projects 10–20% growth in PM roles through 2030. The risk is not displacement — it is being outcompeted by PMs who use AI better.

## Phase 1 Verdict

**The Vietnam PM/DM market is healthy for a 10-year experienced professional.**

### Profile Advantages (real, documented)

1. **International client experience (US/EU):** +15–30% salary premium locally; hard prerequisite for international remote roles. Most locally trained PMs cannot compete here.
2. **Combined PM + DM scope (10 years):** Rare. Increasingly valued as outsourcing companies shift toward product-led models.
3. **English proficiency:** Eliminates most of the local candidate pool at the senior level.
4. **Right market timing:** PM jobs at 3-year high globally; senior talent scarce relative to demand.

### Real Gaps to Address (prioritized)

1. **AI tool fluency** — Urgently needed. Demonstrate hands-on AI integration in workflow and document it visibly.
2. **Formal certifications** — PMP, SAFe, or CSPO appear in 30–40% of DM postings. Low-cost, tangible screening value.
3. **Data/analytics baseline** — SQL and product analytics (Amplitude, Mixpanel) increasingly baseline, especially for product company roles.
4. **Product company portfolio** — If experience is outsourcing/delivery-heavy, product companies may require evidence of vision ownership and discovery work, not just execution.

### Anxiety Calibration

- The market data does not support worst-case fears. A 10-year PM/DM with international client experience and English proficiency sits in the top tier of the local candidate pool.
- The "2-month replacement" security threshold is **achievable** — senior DM roles at outsourcing firms are actively open and this profile fits well.
- The biggest financial upside is the international remote path ($80k–$138k USD/year = 3–5x the local ceiling). Realistic medium-term option, not a fantasy.

### Go/No-Go Decision

**Go.** Data is sufficient to close Phase 1. Proceed to Phase 2 (2–3 recruiter conversations, 2–3 peer conversations) to validate key numbers, then close the career loop and redirect energy fully to Pillar 1.
