# MVA Build Plan

> **What this is:** The first creative execution phase. Build the 4 anchor points that form the Minimum Viable Architecture. Not a full story — just enough for the architect brain to start building with confidence.
>
> **Format for all artifacts:** See `_TREATMENT_FORMAT.md`.
>
> **How sessions work:** Start a creative session → state which step you're working on → AI generates treatment drafts based on your direction → curate until the movie plays → save → check ✅.

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — The Ending Feeling `[ ]`

**Question to answer:** At the very last scene, what does the viewer feel?

**Artifact:** A short treatment (half to one page) of the final moment. Not plot mechanics — the *emotional experience* of watching it end. What the viewer sees, what they feel in the body, what stays with them after it's over.

**Anchor questions to draw from:**
- How did it feel at the end of the Chimera Ant arc?
- How did it feel at the end of Slam Dunk's final game?
- What is the specific emotional flavor the ending should leave?

**Done when:** Reading it back triggers a clear emotional image. Doesn't need to be "right" — needs to be *felt*.

**Output file:** `01_ending.md`

---

### Step 2 — The Protagonist `[ ]`

**Question to answer:** What kind of person makes that ending hit the hardest?

**Artifact:** A character treatment (one page):
- What they want more than anything
- What's fundamentally broken in them
- What makes the audience root for them despite the flaw
- One concrete image that captures their essence (a moment: how they walk into a room, how they react when cornered)

**Done when:** The protagonist feels like a real person with a real wound. Their existence makes the ending from Step 1 feel inevitable and earned.

**Output file:** `02_protagonist.md`

---

### Step 3 — The Theme `[ ]`

**Question to answer:** What is this story fundamentally about?

**Artifact:** One or two sentences. Format: *"This story is about ___."*

**Self-test:**
- Does the ending *prove* the theme?
- Does the protagonist's journey *explore* the theme?

**Done when:** The sentence feels true, not clever. Connects protagonist to ending in a way that feels inevitable.

**Output file:** `03_theme.md`

---

### Step 4 — The System Anchor `[ ]`

**Question to answer:** What one rule governs the world, and how does it make the protagonist's struggle harder?

**Artifact:** A brief treatment of the power system's *core rule* — not the full system. One foundational principle that creates the constraints the protagonist must operate within. Then: one moment where the rule creates an impossible choice for the protagonist.

**Done when:** The rule feels logical, feels consequential, and creates clear tension with the protagonist's want. You can see how it makes the ending harder and more meaningful.

**Output file:** `04_system_anchor.md`

---

## Success / Failure Signals

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Reading all 4 MVA files back-to-back triggers a movie and excitement | ✅ MVA done. Proceed to iterative scene development. |
| One or two files feel weak | Normal. Rewrite using what the strong files revealed. |
| Nothing triggers a movie. Reads like homework. | ⚠️ Format problem. Add more sensory anchors or rethink the starting point. |
| Can't produce Step 1 after genuine effort | 🔴 Honest signal. Reassess the creative direction. |
