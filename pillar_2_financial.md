# Pillar 2 — Financial Resilience Checklist

> **Purpose:** A one-time setup checklist to ensure financial resilience against income disruption, health incidents, and inflation erosion. Once complete, reviewed once per year maximum. Not an ongoing activity.

> **Disclaimer:** This is a personal planning document, not professional financial advice. All specific decisions should be validated against current Vietnam regulations and personal circumstances.

---

## The Three Layers

### Layer 1: Emergency Buffer — ✅ Partially Done
*Handles: job loss, health crisis, sudden large expense*

- [ ] Calculate total monthly living expenses (all-in: rent, food, school, bills, etc.)
- [ ] Target: **12 months** of expenses in liquid savings (12 months, not 6 — father with single income)
- [ ] Verify: is the current bank savings balance above this 12-month target?
- [ ] If yes → Layer 1 is done. Do not touch this portion for investment.
- [ ] If no → Prioritize reaching this target before Layer 2.

**Storage:** Multiple bank savings accounts at 5–7% interest. This is correct for this layer. No changes needed.

---

### Layer 2: Inflation Protection — 🔲 To Do
*Handles: long-term purchasing power erosion*

**Context:** Bank deposits at 5–7% beat Vietnam's average inflation of 3–4%, giving a real return of roughly +1–3%. This is acceptable for the emergency buffer but insufficient for long-term wealth preservation. Equity index funds have historically returned significantly more over 7+ year horizons, with higher short-term volatility.

**Decision checklist:**

- [ ] Calculate: Total savings minus 12 months living expenses = **investable surplus**
- [ ] Start with 20–30% of that surplus allocated to index fund (not a lump sum — spread over 6–12 months)
- [ ] Choose a starting vehicle:
  - **VN30 ETF** (E1VFVN30 on HOSE, or equivalent) — simple, Vietnam-focused, accessible through any local brokerage (SSI, VPS, TCBS, etc.)
  - **International option** (future consideration): S&P 500 or global index via Interactive Brokers or Tiger Brokers — more diversified, less Vietnam concentration risk
- [ ] Open a brokerage account if not already done (15–30 min online process)
- [ ] Set up a recurring monthly purchase — do not try to time the market
- [ ] Hard rule: do not check balance more than twice per year

**Starting allocation principle:**
| Savings portion | Where |
|---|---|
| First 12 months of expenses | Bank savings (stay liquid, do not move) |
| Remaining surplus — first 20–30% | VN30 ETF, spread over 6–12 months |
| Remaining surplus — the rest | Bank savings until comfortable to increase ETF allocation |

---

### Layer 3: Insurance — 🔲 To Do
*Handles: catastrophic health event, inability to work, death*

#### 3a. Health Coverage Audit
- [ ] Review current employer health insurance (BHYT + any private supplement): what does it actually cover?
- [ ] Identify the maximum out-of-pocket in a serious illness scenario
- [ ] If major gap exists: research a private health insurance supplement (Bảo Việt, Prudential, AIA — outpatient/inpatient rider only, no investment component)

#### 3b. Term Life Insurance (not whole life, not ILP)
**What to look for:** Pure risk coverage only. Pays out a lump sum if death or total permanent disability occurs. No savings component. No investment component. Premiums are low.

**What to avoid:** Whole life policies, investment-linked policies (ILPs) sold by agents — these mix bad investment products with insurance at high fees.

- [ ] Run the self-insurance math first:
  - If current savings cover 24+ months of household expenses → self-insurance may be sufficient
  - If savings are below 24 months → term life is worth serious consideration
- [ ] If proceeding: get quotes from 2–3 providers for pure term life coverage (Prudential, Manulife, AIA all offer standalone term products in Vietnam)
- [ ] Compare: annual premium vs. coverage amount vs. covered conditions
- [ ] Decision criteria: if annual premium feels trivial relative to the coverage provided → do it and stop thinking about it

#### 3c. Critical Illness Rider (optional but worth checking)
- [ ] Check if current employer insurance or any existing policy includes critical illness coverage (pays lump sum on diagnosis of cancer, stroke, heart attack, etc.)
- [ ] If not covered: this is the highest-value gap — a serious diagnosis can cost hundreds of millions VND before disability even enters the picture

## Annual Review (1 hour, once per year)

Scheduled alongside the career scan. Do not review more frequently.

- [ ] Is the emergency buffer still at 12 months? (Expenses may have changed)
- [ ] Is the ETF auto-purchase still running?
- [ ] Any major life change that affects insurance needs? (new child, significant salary change, etc.)
- [ ] Reconfirm: nothing else needs attention. Close the loop and return to Pillar 1.

---

## What Not to Do

- **Do not follow financial news or markets** — this feeds anxiety, not returns
- **Do not switch investments based on short-term performance** — the strategy only works if left alone
- **Do not buy whole life / ILP products** from insurance agents — the investment component is a poor deal
- **Do not try to "optimize" beyond this system** — complexity is the enemy of consistency
